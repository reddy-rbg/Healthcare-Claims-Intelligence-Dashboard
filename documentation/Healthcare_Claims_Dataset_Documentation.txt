# Healthcare Claims Power BI Dataset Documentation

## Dataset Source

This dataset is NOT taken from a real company, hospital, insurance provider, or patient database.

It is a synthetically generated healthcare claims dataset created for a Power BI analytics project. The structure is based on a hybrid approach inspired by real-world healthcare payer analytics scenarios and public synthetic healthcare data concepts such as:

1. CMS DE-SynPUF / Medicare Synthetic Public Use Files
   - Used as inspiration for Medicare-style claims, members, diagnosis, and payment analytics.
   - Official source: https://www.cms.gov/data-research/statistics-trends-and-reports/medicare-claims-synthetic-public-use-files

2. Synthea / SyntheticMass synthetic healthcare data
   - Used as inspiration for synthetic patient/member healthcare records and healthcare utilization scenarios.
   - Official source: https://synthea.mitre.org/downloads

The actual dataset files in this project were generated synthetically for practice and business intelligence demonstration. No real patient information, protected health information, or company-confidential data is included.

---

# Project Title

Healthcare Claims Intelligence Dashboard: Cost, Denials, SLA & Provider Risk Analysis

---

# Business Scenario

A healthcare payer wants to monitor claims cost, denial trends, provider performance, SLA compliance, payment delays, and data quality issues.

The dashboard is designed to help leadership and operations teams answer questions such as:

- Which claim types are driving the highest cost?
- Which providers have high denial rates?
- Which regions are missing SLA targets?
- Which claims are delayed or paid late?
- Are there duplicate claims or invalid claim amounts?
- Are out-of-network providers increasing claim cost?
- Which member groups or plan types are driving higher utilization?
- What are the overall data quality issues in the claims process?

- Members

## Recommended Relationships

1. Claims[MemberID] → Members[MemberID]
2. Claims[ProviderID] → Providers[ProviderID]
3. Claims[DiagnosisCode] → Diagnosis[DiagnosisCode]
4. Claims[ClaimType] → SLA_Targets[ClaimType]
5. Claims[ClaimDate] → Date[Date]
6. Payments[ClaimID] → Claims[ClaimID]

Important note:
Claims.csv intentionally contains duplicate ClaimID records for data quality practice. In Power BI, keep the raw Claims table for data quality reporting, and create a cleaned Claims table in Power Query for final dashboard modeling.

# Intentional Data Quality Problems

The dataset includes realistic data quality problems that companies commonly face.

## Problems Included

1. Missing Provider IDs
   - Some claims do not have ProviderID.
   - Business impact: claim cannot be mapped to provider performance.

2. Duplicate Claims
   - Some ClaimID values appear more than once.
   - Business impact: inflated claim count and possible duplicate payment risk.

3. Invalid Claim Amounts
   - Some ClaimAmount values are zero or negative.
   - Business impact: wrong financial reporting and claim validation errors.

4. Blank Denial Reasons
   - Some denied claims do not have a denial reason.
   - Business impact: poor denial analysis and incomplete operational reporting.

5. SLA Missed Claims
   - Some claims have ProcessingDays greater than SLA_Days.
   - Business impact: operational delays and service-level non-compliance.

6. Late Payments
   - Some payments are delayed after claim approval.
   - Business impact: provider dissatisfaction and payment operations backlog.

7. High-Cost Outliers
   - Some claim amounts are unusually high.
   - Business impact: potential audit, review, or fraud investigation.

8. Out-of-Network Impact
   - Out-of-network providers generally have higher costs and denial risk.
   - Business impact: network leakage and cost management issues.
